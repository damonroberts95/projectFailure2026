I was training some colleagues on a part of some software which was important to us collectively, but new to several people. One of the warehouse fellows entered the room, and in his natural discomfort said, "I don't know why I am here. I am just a stupid warehouseman." Instead of thinking before I spoke, I asked, "Do you not want to be here?" and he said yes, so I told him to go. It did not take a few minutes to know this was a mistake. But, at that young state of career, I was not very confident. So, I responded to my own insecurity and frustration to find my road not paved with smiles. I did later apologize to him, and gave him the training in a dedicated session for his role. But, it was a close run thing whether he would respect my youthful ignorance and my inability to take the jostling of the role I was hired for. - Anonymous
